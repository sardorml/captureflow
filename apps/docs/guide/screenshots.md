# Screenshots

A **screenshot** behaves exactly like a recording: capture it,
get an instant link, and share it. Screenshots are perfect when a still image says it
faster than a video — a bug, a design, a quick "see this?"

## Capture a screenshot

1. Trigger a screenshot from the CaptureFlow toolbar.
2. Select a region (or window/screen) the same way you do for a recording.
3. CaptureFlow uploads the image and copies a **share link** to your clipboard.

## The screenshot viewer

Screenshots open on their own share page with:

- A **zoomable** image viewer.
- The same **visibility** model as recordings — public, private, or
  workspace-only (see [Sharing](/guide/sharing#visibility)).
- A **download** action and copy-link button.
- Attribution showing who posted it and when.

## Edit a screenshot

Open the **screenshot editor** to annotate or adjust before (or after) sharing, then
update the title or visibility. Like recordings, screenshots live in your dashboard
under **Screenshots** and count toward your storage quota.

## Screenshot vs. recording — which to use?

| Use a **screenshot** when…   | Use a **recording** when…              |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| One frame tells the story    | You need to show a flow or motion      |
| You want to annotate a still | Narration or a walkthrough helps       |
| Filing a quick visual bug    | Demoing a feature or reproducing steps |
