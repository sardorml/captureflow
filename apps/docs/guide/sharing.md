# Share a recording

Every recording becomes a **share page** at a short URL you can paste anywhere.
Viewers watch in the browser — no account or install required (unless you've
restricted the recording).

## The instant link

When you stop recording, CaptureFlow copies a share link to your clipboard
right away. Open it and you'll see the share viewer: the video with a poster
thumbnail, the title, and the activity sidebar.

## Visibility

Each recording (and screenshot) has a visibility setting you control from the desktop
app, the dashboard, or the share page itself:

| Visibility    | Who can watch                                             |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Public**    | Anyone with the link.                                     |
| **Private**   | Only you (and signed-in teammates you've granted access). |
| **Workspace** | Members of your workspace.                                |

If someone without access opens a restricted link, they're prompted to sign in
or **request access**.

## On the share page

The viewer includes:

- **Playback** with a generated poster image.
- **Reactions** — quick emoji responses.
- **Comments** — threaded discussion in the activity sidebar.
- **View count** — how many times it's been watched.
- **Summary chapters** — auto-generated chapter markers to jump around longer
  recordings.
- **Download** — grab the original file (subject to visibility).

## Edit a recording

From the dashboard or share page, open the **editor** to rename the recording,
adjust its visibility, or manage it. Titles can be auto-suggested, so a fresh
recording isn't just "Untitled."

## Manage everything from the dashboard

The web dashboard is your home base:

- **Recordings** — your full library, with quick visibility and link actions.
- **Screenshots** — your screenshots (see [Screenshots](/guide/screenshots)).
- **Members & workspaces** — invite teammates, manage roles, set who can upload.
- **Devices** — the signed-in recorders on your account.
- **Settings & profile** — workspace name/logo, access policy, your profile.
- **Storage usage** — how much of your quota you've used.
